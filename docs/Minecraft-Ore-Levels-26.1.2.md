## How ore distribution works (why "peak Y" matters)

Most ores use a **triangle distribution**: they're most common at one center Y level and get rarer the farther you go above or below it. That's why "peak Y" is where you should dig — e.g. diamonds exist anywhere from Y -64 to +16, but you'll find *far* more per hour mining at -59 than at -10.

A few ores (iron, copper) generate in **multiple separate batches**, which is why iron has two good spots (deep underground *and* mountain peaks).

## Practical mining tips

- **Diamonds:** Branch-mine at **Y -58** (one above the densest -59 layer) to dodge bedrock and lava pockets while staying in the sweet spot. Bring an iron pickaxe and a water bucket.
- **Fortune III** pickaxe massively boosts diamond, redstone, lapis, coal, emerald, and copper yields (not iron/gold/ancient debris — those drop blocks, use Fortune only on the raw-ore ones).
- **Iron early game:** don't dig deep — just scan **mountain cliffs** for exposed iron at Y 80+.
- **Ancient Debris:** mine at **Y 15** using the "bed trick" or TNT in a straight tunnel; it's blast-resistant so it survives explosions that clear the netherrack around it.
